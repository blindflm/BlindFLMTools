Blind FLM

The Blind Fingerstroke-Level Model (Blind FLM) is an extension of the Keystroke-Level Model (KLM) that numerically predict the time it takes an expert visually impaired user to accomlish a task on a touch-based smartphone. Blind FLM adapts orignal KLM operators and introduce new ones to represent unique interactions of users who are visually impaired. The heauristic rules were also modified to update the placement of the mental operator considering the newly adapted operators. In Blind FLM tasks are constructed usign the extended operators and heuristic rules. Using the model, the time it takes a user who is visually impaired can be predicted by adding up the unit times of the operations necessary to complete the task. Blind FLM's performance was validated where it was found to be able to accuratly predict the performance of exprt users who are visually impaired on the iPhone.
